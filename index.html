Rohan Chitnis

I am a fourth-year undergraduate at UC Berkeley in the department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences. In Fall 2016, I will begin my PhD at MIT EECS, within CSAIL.

News:
In March 2016, I was awarded an NSF GRFP Fellowship (accepted) and an NDSEG Fellowship (declined).

In January 2016, I was selected as one of 40 Finalists for the Hertz Fellowship, a highly reputable fellowship for student researchers in the physical, biological, and engineering sciences.

In December 2015, I was selected as the Runner-up for the 2016 Computing Research Association (CRA) Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Award (Male, PhD-granting institution).

Bio:
I conduct research primarily in robotics/AI, with Professor Pieter Abbeel's Robot Learning Lab. My work is in hierarchical task and motion planning, which seeks to get robots to execute tasks that require a long sequence of steps, like doing laundry. I have recently been exploring reinforcement learning techniques for improving the performance of these systems.

I also dabble in NLP (Natural Language Processing). I've done some work in neural machine translation with John DeNero as part of the Oscii Lab.

I enjoy being a TA for CS classes. The culture of collaboration in the Berkeley CS community is one of my favorite aspects of being a student, and I'm happy to be doing my part. I've thus far been a TA for courses on introductory computer science, computer architecture, and artificial intelligence. This semester, I'm excited to be a TA for the course on machine learning!
