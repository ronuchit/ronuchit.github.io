Rohan Chitnis

I am a fourth-year undergraduate at UC Berkeley in the department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences.

I was recently selected as the Runner-up for the 2016 Computing Research Association (CRA) Outstanding Undergraduate Researcher Award (Male, PhD-granting institution)!

I conduct research primarily in robotics/AI, with Professor Pieter Abbeel's Robot Learning Lab. My main interests are in hierarchical planning, which seeks to get robots to execute tasks that require a long sequence of steps, like setting a dinner table. I have recently been exploring reinforcement learning techniques for improving the performance of hierarchical planning systems. Check out my publications for more information.

I also dabble in NLP (Natural Language Processing). I've done some work in neural machine translation with John DeNero as part of the Oscii Lab.

I've taken some advanced classes and participated in a couple of hackathons, so I've got a few interesting projects you can check out.

I enjoy being a TA for CS classes. The culture of collaboration in the Berkeley CS community is one of my favorite aspects of being a student, and I'm happy to be doing my part. I've thus far been a TA for courses on introductory computer science, computer architecture, and artificial intelligence. Next semester, I'm excited to be a TA for the course on machine learning!

My primary hobby and source of stress relief is running. I ran my fifth half-marathon in September 2015. I also enjoy reading. Here are some good books.
